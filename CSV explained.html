CSV Files: The Simple Way

What is a CSV? (The Non-Tech Talk)

A CSV (Comma Separated Values) file is just a super simple list. Think of it as a spreadsheet stripped down to the bare essentials: words and commas. Our tools use them because they are the cleanest way to feed information (like your vocabulary lists) directly into the app, one item at a time.

Step 1: Prep Your List (The Golden Rule) üìù

**The Golden Rule:** Everything starts in **Column A**. What you put there is what the tool will use as the primary item.

Tool Requirements Breakdown:

  • üë§ Classroom Management: Only needs **Column A** (Student Names). You can leave the rest blank!
  • üéÆ Hangman / Games: Only needs **Column A** (The Word/Phrase to guess). Anything in Column B or C is ignored.

Focus: The Vocab Trainer Breakdown üí°

The Vocab Trainer is the most powerful tool, using up to **three columns** for robust practice. The app randomly cycles between these three items:

  • **Column A (Word):** The main vocabulary item.
  • **Column B (Definition):** A short explanation of the word.
  • **Column C (Example):** A full sentence showing the word in context.

‚ú® **Pro Tip: Use AI to save time!** You can quickly ask an AI (like Google's Gemini, ChatGPT, etc.) to convert your Column A words into a full 3-column CSV format instantly. This is the fastest way to build rich vocab lists!

Step 2: Save as a CSV! üíæ

This is the magic part. Instead of saving it as a regular spreadsheet (`.xlsx` or `.gsheet`), you need to choose the "CSV" option when exporting.

In almost every program, follow these three clicks:

  1. Click **File** in the top menu.
  2. Find **Download** or **Export**.
  3. Select **Comma Separated Values (.csv)**.

You're Done! üéâ

That new `.csv` file is now ready to be loaded directly into the Vocab Trainer, Hangman Game, or any other tool that needs a simple word list. Easy peasy!
